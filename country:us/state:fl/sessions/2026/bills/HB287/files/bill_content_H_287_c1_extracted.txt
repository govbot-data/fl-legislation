151 case plan and having their parenting rights restored. As a
152 result of being the person ultimately responsible for the care
153 of a child who is removed from the home of his or her biological
154 parents, owners and operators of family foster homes may incur
155 the ill will of the biological parents, thus making such owners

157 revenge. If such identifying and location information is
158 released, the safety of the owners and operators of family
159 foster homes and all children living with them could be
160 seriously jeopardized. Additionally, exposing such information
161 of family foster home applicants and their families may cause a
162 chilling effect on the number of applications received because
163 of the potential exposure of sensitive personal information,
164 thereby reducing the number of foster families willing to foster
165 children. The Legislature further finds that the harm that may
166 result from the release of such personal identifying and
167 location information outweighs any public benefit that may be
168 derived from the disclosure of the information.
